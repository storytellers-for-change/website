About

Stories for Change: Interactive Storytelling for Social Impact is a one-day workshop uniting researchers, designers, and activists to prototype interactive narratives that deeply engage audiences on issues of poverty, inequality, and climate resilience.

We move beyond data and awareness: by weaving choice and consequence into story, we empower participants to shift from passive spectators into active agents of change. This workshop investigates interactive storytelling as a powerful tool for enhancing public engagement with pressing social issues, such as poverty, inequality, and climate change.

Themes

The workshop revolves around three core themes, each designed to deepen our understanding of interactive storytelling’s role in social impact:

  • Transforming Awareness to Action

    How can narrative interactions move participants from empathy to engagement and real-world solutions?

  • Fostering Perspective Shifts

    Which interactive techniques effectively challenge assumptions and open new viewpoints?

  • Building Culturally Relevant Narratives

    What methods ensure stories resonate authentically across diverse cultural contexts?

Call for Abstracts

Please provide a 1–2 page abstract describing a real-world case where storytelling played—or could have played—a key role. Include:

  • Context/Setting: Outline the situation and why a narrative intervention mattered.
  • Interactive Approach: Describe your storytelling or media design techniques.
  • Position Statement: Was it successful? If not, what barriers arose? If yes, what drove success?
  • Relevance to Themes: Indicate which theme(s) your example addresses.

We seek contributions from all fields—research, design, activism, education, and community practice. Selection is based on thematic balance and contribution quality. Accepted submissions will be shared on the workshop site beforehand.
